Tourist Spots & Adventures

Burnham Park

Burnham Park in Baguio City is a 32.84-hectare urban green space designed by American architect Daniel Burnham, featuring gardens, recreational areas and a man-made lake.

Famous Lion's Head in Baguio

The Lion's Head in Baguio City is a 40-foot iconic landmark carved out of limestone along Kennon Road, symbolizing the city's connection to the Lions Club since its completion in 1972.

Laperal white house

The Laperal White House in Baguio City is a historic, Victorian-style mansion built in the 1930s, now home to Joseph's, a fine dining restaurant serving Continental and French cuisine, while retaining its eerie reputation as one of the Philippines' most haunted sites.

Locale Products

Baguio Meringue

Meringue in Baguio is a popular sweet treat often found in local bakeries like Good Shepherd Bakeshop, making it a perfect pasalubong (gift) for friends and family.

Price: ₱150

Peanut Brittle

Peanut brittle in Baguio City is a classic, crunchy, and sweet treat made from peanuts and caramelized sugar, often sold as a popular pasalubong (gift) or souvenir from the city.

Price: ₱250

Baguio Wines

Baguio City offers a selection of wines, particularly at local wine bars and restaurants, catering to those looking to unwind or enjoy a fine dining experience amidst the city's cool climate..

Price: ₱300 for 3pcs


Assorted Souvenirs

Baguio City offers a variety of souvenirs, including woven crafts, wood carvings, strawberry goodies, peanut brittle, ube treats, and pasalubong items, showcasing the city's culture and local products.

Price: ₱70 per pcs


Traditional Cordilleran Textiles

Traditional Cordilleran textiles in Baguio showcase vibrant, handwoven fabrics featuring intricate designs and patterns, reflecting the rich cultural heritage of the indigenous Cordillera tribes, often used in clothing, accessories, and home decor.

Price: ₱130


Baguio T-shirt

A Baguio t-shirt is a casual, often souvenir-inspired shirt featuring designs or logos related to the city, its culture, or iconic landmarks like the Burnham Park or the Lion's Head..

Price: ₱250


Freshly-Picked Strawberries

Baguio strawberries are a sweet and juicy variety grown in the city's cool climate, often enjoyed fresh, made into jams, or used in local delicacies like strawberry shortcake and pastries. They're a popular pasalubong (gift) and a taste of Baguio's agricultural charm.

Price: ₱180


Strawberry Jam

Strawberry jam in Baguio is a sweet and tangy spread made from the city's famous strawberries, often homemade or locally produced, and enjoyed on toast, pastries, or as a pasalubong.

Price: ₱140
